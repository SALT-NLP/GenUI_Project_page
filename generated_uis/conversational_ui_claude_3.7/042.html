How to Develop Your Financial Projections

  1. Start with Revenue Forecasting

    • Begin by estimating your customer acquisition rate
    • Determine your pricing strategy and expected revenue per customer
    • Consider different revenue streams if applicable

  2. Project Your Expenses

    • List all fixed costs (rent, salaries, subscriptions)
    • Estimate variable costs tied to production/service delivery
    • Don't forget one-time costs like equipment purchases

  3. Develop Cash Flow Projections

    • This shows when you'll need funding and when you'll become profitable
    • Pay special attention to your "runway" (how long before you run out of cash)

  4. Create Multiple Scenarios

    • Best case: Rapid adoption, higher pricing power
    • Realistic case: Moderate growth aligned with industry averages
    • Conservative case: Slower adoption, pricing pressure

  5. Validate Your Assumptions

    • Research industry benchmarks for key metrics
    • Test assumptions with potential customers or industry experts
    • Review and adjust quarterly based on actual performance
