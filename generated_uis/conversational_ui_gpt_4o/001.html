Absolutely! Spanish verb conjugation is the process of changing a verb form to provide information about who is doing the action and when it is happening (tense and subject). Here's a breakdown of the basics:

1. Infinitive Verbs

Spanish infinitive verbs end in -ar, -er, or -ir, such as:

  • hablar (to speak)
  • comer (to eat)
  • vivir (to live)

2. Subjects (Pronouns)

Conjugations vary depending on who is performing the action:

Subject Pronoun Meaning
yo I
tú you (informal)
él / ella / usted he / she / you (formal)
nosotros/as we
vosotros/as you all (informal, Spain)
ellos/as / ustedes they / you all (formal in Spain; all in Latin America)

3. Present Tense Conjugation (Examples)

For -ar verbs (e.g., hablar):

Pronoun Conjugation
yo hablo
tú hablas
él/ella/usted habla
nosotros/as hablamos
vosotros/as habláis
ellos/as/ustedes hablan

For -er verbs (e.g., comer):

Pronoun Conjugation
yo como
tú comes
él/ella/usted come
nosotros/as comemos
vosotros/as coméis
ellos/as/ustedes comen

For -ir verbs (e.g., vivir):

Pronoun Conjugation
yo vivo
tú vives
él/ella/usted vive
nosotros/as vivimos
vosotros/as vivís
ellos/as/ustedes viven

4. Irregular Verbs

Some verbs don't follow standard patterns. Examples:

  • ser (to be): yo soy, tú eres, él es...
  • ir (to go): yo voy, tú vas, él va...

5. Tenses

Conjugations also change based on time:

  • Present: hablo (I speak)
  • Past (preterite): hablé (I spoke)
  • Future: hablaré (I will speak)
  • Imperfect: hablaba (I used to speak)
