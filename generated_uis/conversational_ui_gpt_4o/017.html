To identify key factors influencing Customer Lifetime Value (CLV) and develop actionable insights for increasing repeat purchases in your e-commerce business, you can adopt a comprehensive analytical approach. Here's a suggested framework:

1. Data Preparation and Cleaning

  • Consolidate data: Ensure the dataset includes all relevant variables (transaction details, demographics, browsing behavior, post-purchase surveys).
  • Clean the data: Remove duplicates, handle missing values, and standardize variables (e.g., date formats, categorical variables).

2. Exploratory Data Analysis (EDA)

  • Descriptive analysis: Get an overview of the data with basic statistics (mean, median, etc.), distribution of key variables (purchase frequency, customer age, etc.), and visualizations (histograms, boxplots, etc.).
  • Segmentation analysis: Explore customer segmentation based on demographics, purchase behavior, browsing activity, etc. Use clustering methods like K-means to identify customer groups (e.g., high-value vs. low-value customers).

3. Customer Segmentation

  • Use RFM (Recency, Frequency, Monetary) analysis to classify customers based on their purchasing behavior.
  • Cluster analysis: Use K-means clustering or hierarchical clustering to group customers by similar characteristics such as purchasing patterns, demographics, and browsing behavior.
  • This segmentation will help you understand the behavior of high-value customers and target them more effectively.

4. Feature Engineering

Create new features that might help in predicting CLV:

  • Time-based features: For example, the time since the last purchase (recency), total number of purchases (frequency), and total spend (monetary).
  • Customer engagement: Metrics such as website visits per month, average session duration, and items viewed before purchasing.
  • Post-purchase survey responses: Sentiment analysis or rating of product satisfaction to understand how likely they are to repeat purchases.

5. Predicting Customer Lifetime Value (CLV)

To estimate CLV, you'll want to build predictive models that incorporate transaction history, customer demographics, and browsing behavior:

  • CLV Formula-based approach: A basic method involves the formula:
    [
    CLV = (Average Purchase Value) \times (Purchase Frequency) \times (Customer Lifespan)
    ]

  • Survival analysis: You could use Cox Proportional Hazards or Kaplan-Meier curves to model customer churn and retention rates, providing a more precise estimate of a customer's lifetime in your business.

6. Identifying Key Drivers of CLV

  • Correlation analysis: Identify features that strongly correlate with CLV using correlation matrices or pairwise comparisons.
  • Feature importance: Use techniques like Random Forests or Gradient Boosting to determine which factors (e.g., demographics, browsing behavior, satisfaction) most influence CLV.
  • Causal inference: You might want to assess the causal relationships between factors such as satisfaction (from survey responses) and repeat purchases. A/B testing could help here if you want to evaluate the effectiveness of certain strategies.

7. Actionable Insights for Increasing Repeat Purchases

Based on the analysis, you can derive strategies aimed at improving CLV and repeat purchases:

  • Customer retention programs: Identify customers with high potential for retention (based on your CLV model) and target them with loyalty programs or personalized marketing.
  • Personalized marketing campaigns: Use customer segmentation to send targeted offers based on customer preferences, demographics, or browsing behavior.
  • Optimizing website and UX: Identify browsing behaviors that correlate with higher purchases and optimize the website experience (e.g., product recommendations, simplified checkout process).
  • Post-purchase engagement: Encourage repeat purchases by following up with personalized post-purchase emails, product recommendations, or incentives (e.g., discount offers).
  • Survey responses analysis: Utilize survey insights to improve customer experience or product offerings. For example, improving customer service or addressing common pain points (long shipping times, product quality).

8. Visualization and Reporting

  • Visualize key insights such as CLV distribution, factors influencing CLV, and customer segments using tools like Power BI or Tableau.
  • Regularly monitor changes in CLV and repeat purchase rates after implementing strategies, using dashboards to keep track of business impact over time.

9. Iterate and Optimize

  • Test & learn: Implement A/B testing for different marketing strategies or product offerings and monitor how they impact CLV and repeat purchase rates.
  • Model refinement: Continuously refine your predictive models as more data comes in to improve accuracy over time.

By combining these approaches, you'll be able to identify actionable insights that can directly influence customer behavior, drive repeat purchases, and optimize your CLV.
