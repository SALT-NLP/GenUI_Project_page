That sounds like an exciting project! For a fitness tracking app interface, you want to focus on clarity, ease of use, and motivating visuals. Here’s a guide on key features and design elements you might want to consider for your app:

1. Splash Screen

  • A welcoming screen that includes your app’s logo and possibly a motivational tagline.
  • Simple, clean design with vibrant colors that give off energy.

2. Onboarding Screens

  • Walkthrough for new users to help them understand the app features.
  • Allow users to set their fitness goals (e.g., steps, calories, workout types, or weight).

3. Dashboard/Home Screen

  • Summary Section: A quick overview of daily stats (steps, calories burned, workout time).
  • Quick Stats: Display key metrics like heart rate, distance traveled, and active minutes.
  • Activity Feed: A list of activities for the day or week.
  • Motivational Element: Use visuals like progress bars, badges, or weekly/monthly challenges to motivate users.
  • Quick Access: Buttons to start a workout, check progress, or view a calendar of activity.

4. Activity Tracker Screen

  • Workout Details: Show time elapsed, calories burned, distance traveled, etc., during a workout.
  • Real-time Updates: Provide real-time stats (e.g., pace, heart rate, calories) while the user is working out.
  • End Workout Button: Clearly visible, and allow the user to save the activity.

5. Progress Tracker

  • Graphical representation of progress over time (e.g., charts for steps, calories, weight, or workout consistency).
  • Milestones and rewards: Show users when they’ve hit goals like daily step targets or weekly workout goals.
  • Historical Data: Allow users to look at past achievements or trends (like weekly steps, weight over time, or best times).

6. Challenges and Achievements

  • Allow users to participate in challenges (steps per day, weight loss, etc.) to make fitness more engaging.
  • Show badges or achievements for completed challenges or personal records.

7. Settings/Profile Screen

  • User can set personal details like age, weight, fitness goals, and preferred workouts.
  • Sync with health data sources (Apple Health, Google Fit, wearables, etc.)
  • Privacy settings for shared progress or social integration.

8. Social Integration (Optional)

  • Let users share their progress or challenges with friends or a community.
  • Create a leaderboard or follow friends’ progress.

9. Notifications

  • Reminder notifications to stay active, drink water, or meet fitness goals.
  • Motivational notifications for milestones achieved.

10. Color Scheme & Typography

  • Colors: Bright and energetic colors like blues, oranges, or greens. You might also want to use a contrast of dark/light themes.
  • Typography: Bold and easy-to-read fonts for activity stats and notifications.

11. Usability

  • Easy-to-use buttons, icons, and clear labels.
  • Large touch targets (especially for quick actions like starting a workout).
  • Simple navigation with a bottom or side menu for easy access to key sections.
