Great! Creating financial projections is a crucial step for understanding your startup’s financial future and attracting investors. To get started, you'll typically want to build projections for the next 3 to 5 years, including:

1. Revenue Projections

Estimate how much money your startup will make:

  • Sales forecast: Units sold × Price per unit.
  • Consider growth rates, market size, customer acquisition, and seasonality.

2. Cost of Goods Sold (COGS)

Estimate the direct costs associated with producing your product/service.

3. Operating Expenses

Include:

  • Salaries and wages
  • Rent and utilities
  • Marketing and sales
  • R&D
  • Admin costs
  • Other recurring expenses

4. Capital Expenditures (CapEx)

Any major investments in equipment, tech, or infrastructure.

5. Cash Flow Statement

Shows inflow and outflow of cash monthly or quarterly:

  • Operations
  • Investments
  • Financing

6. Profit and Loss (P&L) Statement

Includes:

  • Revenue
  • Expenses
  • EBITDA
  • Net profit

7. Balance Sheet

Assets = Liabilities + Equity
