    Create a Compelling Subject Line

    0/70 characters
    A good subject line is clear, specific, and creates curiosity or urgency.

    Subject Line Best Practices

    • Keep it short and sweet - Aim for 30-50 characters to ensure it displays fully on mobile devices.
    • Create a sense of urgency - Words like "limited time," "today only," or "ending soon" can increase open rates.
    • Ask a question - Questions engage readers and make them curious about the answer inside.
    • Use numbers - "5 Ways to..." or "3 Tips for..." helps set clear expectations.
    • Avoid spam trigger words - Terms like "free," "guarantee," and excessive punctuation (!!!) can trigger spam filters.

    Write Your Newsletter Content

    Some tips to consider:

    • Begin with a friendly greeting
    • Share your most important news first
    • Break up text with subheadings
    • Include a clear call-to-action
    • End with a personal sign-off

    Content Writing Tips

    • Focus on one main topic - Newsletters with a single focus perform better than those covering multiple topics.
    • Use the inverted pyramid - Start with the most important information, then provide details, and end with additional context.
    • Keep paragraphs short - Aim for 2-3 sentences per paragraph for better readability, especially on mobile.
    • Include a clear CTA - Every newsletter should have one primary action you want readers to take.
    • Use a conversational tone - Write as if you're speaking to a friend to create connection.

    Template Design Tips

    • Prioritize mobile responsiveness - Over 60% of emails are opened on mobile devices.
    • Use white space effectively - Generous spacing improves readability and focus.
    • Limit your color palette - Stick to 2-3 colors that align with your brand.
    • Make CTAs stand out - Use contrasting colors and clear button shapes.
    • Keep it consistent - Use the same template for all your newsletters to build recognition.

    Final Checklist

    • Check for typos and grammar errors - Proofread carefully or use a tool like Grammarly.
    • Verify all links work - Click each link to ensure they direct to the correct pages.
    • Test on multiple devices - Check how your newsletter appears on desktop, tablet, and mobile.
    • Include unsubscribe option - This is legally required in most countries.
    • Send a test email - Before sending to your list, send a test to yourself.
