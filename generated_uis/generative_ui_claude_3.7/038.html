Water has an AX₂E₂ electron geometry, resulting in a bent molecular geometry with a bond angle of 104.5° (less than the ideal tetrahedral angle of 109.5° due to lone pair repulsion).

electric_bolt Electronegativity & Polarity

Oxygen (EN: 3.44) is more electronegative than hydrogen (EN: 2.20), creating polar O-H bonds. Combined with the bent geometry, this gives water a permanent dipole moment of 1.85 D.

This self-ionization is responsible for the pH scale, with pure water having a pH of 7 at 25°C.
