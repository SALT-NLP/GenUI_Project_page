Original Content

English
Introduction to Patient Assessment

This guide provides a structured approach to patient assessment in rural health settings. A thorough assessment is essential for accurate diagnosis and appropriate treatment planning.

Always begin by introducing yourself and explaining what you will be doing. Ensure patient privacy and comfort throughout the assessment.

Initial Observations

Before beginning the formal assessment, observe the patient's general appearance, including:

  • Level of consciousness
  • Breathing pattern and effort
  • Skin color and appearance
  • Posture and movement
  • Facial expressions indicating pain or distress

Note any signs of respiratory distress or shock that require immediate intervention.

Assessment Sequence

Follow this sequence for a complete assessment:

  1. Obtain vital signs
  2. Perform a focused history
  3. Conduct a physical examination
  4. Document findings
  5. Develop an assessment and plan

Translation

Spanish
Introducción a la Evaluación del Paciente

Esta guía proporciona un enfoque estructurado para la evaluación de pacientes en entornos de salud rural. Una evaluación completa es esencial para un diagnóstico preciso y una planificación adecuada del tratamiento.

Siempre comience presentándose y explicando lo que va a hacer. Asegure la privacidad y comodidad del paciente durante toda la evaluación.

Observaciones Iniciales

Antes de comenzar la evaluación formal, observe la apariencia general del paciente, incluyendo:

  • Nivel de conciencia
  • Patrón y esfuerzo respiratorio
  • Color y apariencia de la piel
  • Postura y movimiento
  • Expresiones faciales que indican dolor o angustia

Observe cualquier signo de dificultad respiratoria o shock que requiera intervención inmediata.

Secuencia de Evaluación

Siga esta secuencia para una evaluación completa:

  1. Obtener signos vitales
  2. Realizar una historia clínica enfocada
  3. Realizar un examen físico
  4. Documentar hallazgos
  5. Desarrollar una evaluación y plan

Medical Glossary

Respiratory distress
Difficulty breathing, often characterized by rapid or labored breathing.
Spanish: Dificultad respiratoria
K'iche': K'ax uxlanem
Garifuna: Hadügüra anihein
Shock
Inadequate blood flow to body tissues, often indicated by low blood pressure, rapid heart rate, and altered mental status.
Spanish: Shock / Choque
K'iche': Xib'irikil
Garifuna: Adüdüha
Vital signs
Measurement of body temperature, pulse rate, respiration rate, and blood pressure.
Spanish: Signos vitales
K'iche': Retal k'aslemal
Garifuna: Abürühan ibágari

Cultural Notes

Patient Privacy
In many indigenous communities, privacy concepts differ from Western norms. Consider gender-specific care providers and family presence during assessments.
Physical Contact
Explain examination procedures before touching patients. In some communities, direct eye contact may be considered disrespectful.
