Interpretation

The analysis shows a statistically significant positive relationship between green space area and mental health scores, with every additional square meter of green space associated with a 0.025 point increase in mental health score (p < 0.001), controlling for demographic factors.

Distance to parks shows a significant negative relationship with mental health scores, suggesting that proximity to parks is beneficial for mental health outcomes.

Among confounding variables, income and education level show strong positive associations with mental health scores, while age shows a small negative association. Gender does not show a statistically significant relationship in this model.
