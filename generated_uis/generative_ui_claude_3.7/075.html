Dr. Emma Richardson

Director, Climate Policy Institute

Dr. Emma Richardson is a leading expert in international climate policy with over 15 years of experience advising governments, NGOs, and multinational organizations on effective climate action strategies.

She holds a Ph.D. in Environmental Policy from Oxford University and has published extensively on carbon pricing mechanisms, international climate negotiations, and just transition frameworks.

Prior to joining the Climate Policy Institute, Dr. Richardson served as a senior advisor to the UNFCCC and led climate policy development for several European governments. Her work has been instrumental in shaping climate legislation across multiple countries.

At EcoFuture 2023, Dr. Richardson will deliver the opening keynote on the evolving landscape of climate policy and the path toward meaningful global action in the coming decade.

James Wilson

CEO, Greentech Innovations

James Wilson is a visionary entrepreneur and engineer who has dedicated his career to advancing renewable energy technologies. As the founder and CEO of Greentech Innovations, he leads a team developing breakthrough solutions in solar, wind, and energy storage.

With 12 patents to his name, James has revolutionized aspects of photovoltaic efficiency and grid integration systems. His innovations have been implemented in renewable energy projects across five continents.

Before founding Greentech Innovations, James worked as a lead engineer at Tesla and served as a renewable energy advisor to several governments. He holds a Master's in Electrical Engineering from MIT and an MBA from Stanford.

At EcoFuture 2023, James will moderate the panel on Renewable Energy Transition, bringing his practical expertise and industry insights to this critical discussion.

Our Sustainability Commitment

EcoFuture Conference is designed with sustainability at its core. We implement comprehensive measures to minimize our environmental footprint while maximizing positive impact.

recycling

Zero Waste

Our conference implements a comprehensive waste reduction strategy, with a goal of diverting 95% of waste from landfills through recycling, composting, and thoughtful procurement.

restaurant

Sustainable Catering

All food served is plant-forward, locally sourced, and served with minimal packaging. We work with vendors committed to ethical and sustainable practices.

bolt

Carbon Neutral

We measure and offset all emissions associated with the event, including travel, and invest in verified carbon reduction projects that benefit local communities.

public

Digital First

We minimize paper use through digital programs, presentations, and communications, reducing resource consumption while enhancing accessibility.
