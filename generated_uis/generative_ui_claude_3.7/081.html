Understanding Credit Scores

A credit score is a number that shows how likely you are to pay back money you borrow. It helps lenders decide whether to give you a loan and what interest rate to charge.

Why Your Credit Score Matters

Your credit score can affect:

  • Whether you can get a loan for a car or house
  • How much interest you pay on loans and credit cards
  • Your ability to rent an apartment
  • Sometimes, your job applications

What Makes Up Your Credit Score

Your credit score is based on several factors:

  • Payment History (35%) - Whether you pay bills on time
  • Amount Owed (30%) - How much debt you have compared to your credit limits
  • Length of Credit History (15%) - How long you've had credit accounts
  • New Credit (10%) - How many new accounts you've opened recently
  • Types of Credit (10%) - The mix of credit accounts you have

How to Check Your Credit Score

You can get a free copy of your credit report once a year from each of the three major credit bureaus at AnnualCreditReport.com. Many banks and credit card companies also offer free credit score monitoring.

Entendiendo las Puntuaciones de Crédito

Una puntuación de crédito es un número que muestra qué tan probable es que pagues el dinero que pides prestado. Ayuda a los prestamistas a decidir si darte un préstamo y qué tasa de interés cobrarte.

Por Qué Tu Puntuación de Crédito es Importante

Tu puntuación de crédito puede afectar:

  • Si puedes obtener un préstamo para un auto o casa
  • Cuánto interés pagas en préstamos y tarjetas de crédito
  • Tu capacidad para alquilar un apartamento
  • A veces, tus solicitudes de empleo
