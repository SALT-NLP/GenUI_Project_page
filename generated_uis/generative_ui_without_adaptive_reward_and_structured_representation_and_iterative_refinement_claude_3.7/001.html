Spanish Verb Conjugation Guide

Understanding Spanish Verb Conjugation

Verb conjugation in Spanish is the process of changing the form of a verb to indicate who is performing the action (the subject) and when the action is happening (the tense and mood). Unlike English, where conjugation is relatively simple (e.g., "I walk," "he walks"), Spanish verbs change significantly based on the subject, mood, and tense.

Conjugación verbal en español es el proceso de cambiar la forma de un verbo para indicar quién realiza la acción (el sujeto) y cuándo ocurre la acción (el tiempo verbal y el modo). A diferencia del inglés, donde la conjugación es relativamente simple, los verbos españoles cambian significativamente según el sujeto, el modo y el tiempo.

Regular vs. Irregular Verbs

Spanish verbs are broadly categorized into two groups: regular and irregular.

  • Verbos Regulares: These verbs follow predictable patterns based on their infinitive ending (-ar, -er, or -ir). Once you learn the rules for each ending, you can conjugate thousands of regular verbs.
  • Verbos Irregulares: These verbs do not follow the standard patterns. They often have stem changes or unique endings. Common irregular verbs include ser (to be), estar (to be), tener (to have), venir (to come), ir (to go), and hacer (to do/make). These must often be memorized.

Infinitive Endings

All Spanish verbs in their base form (the infinitive) end in one of three ways:

  • -ar verbs (e.g., hablar - to speak)
  • -er verbs (e.g., comer - to eat)
  • -ir verbs (e.g., vivir - to live)

To conjugate a regular verb, you typically remove the infinitive ending (-ar, -er, or -ir) to find the verb stem, and then add the appropriate ending for the subject, tense, and mood.

Subject Pronouns

The subject pronouns indicate who is performing the action. They are:

  • Yo (I)
  • Tú (You - informal singular)
  • Él / Ella / Usted (He / She / You - formal singular)
  • Nosotros / Nosotras (We - masculine/feminine)
  • Vosotros / Vosotras (You - informal plural, used mainly in Spain)
  • Ellos / Ellas / Ustedes (They - masculine/feminine / You - formal plural)

Moods (Modos)

Mood indicates the speaker's attitude towards the action. The main moods in Spanish are:

  • Indicativo (Indicative): Used for factual statements, descriptions, and actions that are considered real or certain. Most common mood.
  • Subjuntivo (Subjunctive): Used to express subjectivity, desires, doubts, emotions, possibilities, and non-factual or hypothetical situations.
  • Imperativo (Imperative): Used to give commands or orders.

Tenses (Tiempos Verbales)

Tense indicates when the action takes place. Within the Indicative mood, some common tenses include:

  • Presente (Present): Used for actions happening now, habitual actions, general truths, or future actions (with context).
  • Pretérito Perfecto Simple (Simple Past / Preterite): Used for completed actions in the past with a definite beginning and end.
  • Pretérito Imperfecto (Imperfect): Used for ongoing, habitual, or descriptive actions in the past without a specific beginning or end.
  • Futuro Simple (Simple Future): Used for actions that will happen in the future.
  • Condicional Simple (Simple Conditional): Used to express hypothetical actions or possibilities ("would" do something).

There are also compound tenses, formed using a conjugated form of the auxiliary verb haber (to have) and the past participle of the main verb (e.g., Pretérito Perfecto Compuesto - Present Perfect: he hablado - I have spoken).

Example Conjugation: Present Indicative

Here are examples of regular verb conjugations in the Present Indicative tense:

-ar verbs: Hablar (to speak)

Pronoun Conjugation English
Yo hablo I speak
Tú hablas You speak
Él/Ella/Usted habla He/She/You speaks
Nosotros/Nosotras hablamos We speak
Vosotros/Vosotras habláis You speak (Spain)
Ellos/Ellas/Ustedes hablan They/You speak

-er verbs: Comer (to eat)

Pronoun Conjugation English
Yo como I eat
Tú comes You eat
Él/Ella/Usted come He/She/You eats
Nosotros/Nosotras comemos We eat
Vosotros/Vosotras coméis You eat (Spain)
Ellos/Ellas/Ustedes comen They/You eat

-ir verbs: Vivir (to live)

Pronoun Conjugation English
Yo vivo I live
Tú vives You live
Él/Ella/Usted vive He/She/You lives
Nosotros/Nosotras vivimos We live
Vosotros/Vosotras vivís You live (Spain)
Ellos/Ellas/Ustedes viven They/You live

Notice how the endings change depending on the subject and the infinitive ending (-ar, -er, -ir). Mastering these patterns and the irregular verbs is key to speaking Spanish fluently.
