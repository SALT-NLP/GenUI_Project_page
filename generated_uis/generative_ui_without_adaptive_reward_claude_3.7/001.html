Spanish Verb Conjugation Guide

A comprehensive guide to understanding and mastering Spanish verb conjugation

La conjugación de verbos es una parte fundamental del idioma español. A diferencia del inglés, el español tiene muchas formas verbales diferentes que cambian según la persona, el tiempo y el modo.

Verb conjugation is a fundamental part of the Spanish language. Unlike English, Spanish has many different verb forms that change according to person, tense, and mood.

This guide will help you understand the patterns and rules of Spanish verb conjugation, starting with the basics and progressing to more complex forms.

The Three Verb Types in Spanish

Spanish verbs are categorized into three types based on their endings in the infinitive form:

-AR Verbs

Verbs ending in -ar like hablar (to speak), caminar (to walk), estudiar (to study).

Example: hablar (to speak)

Infinitive: hablar

Root: habl-

-ER Verbs

Verbs ending in -er like comer (to eat), beber (to drink), leer (to read).

Example: comer (to eat)

Infinitive: comer

Root: com-

-IR Verbs

Verbs ending in -ir like vivir (to live), escribir (to write), abrir (to open).

Example: vivir (to live)

Infinitive: vivir

Root: viv-

Present Tense Conjugation (Presente)

To conjugate a regular verb in the present tense, you remove the infinitive ending (-ar, -er, or -ir) and add the appropriate ending for the subject.

-AR Verbs
-ER Verbs
-IR Verbs

Present Tense: -AR Verbs

Example: hablar (to speak)

Subject Ending Conjugation Translation
yo -o hablo I speak
tú -as hablas you speak (informal)
él/ella/usted -a habla he/she speaks, you speak (formal)
nosotros/nosotras -amos hablamos we speak
vosotros/vosotras -áis habláis you all speak (informal, Spain)
ellos/ellas/ustedes -an hablan they speak, you all speak (formal/Latin America)

Example Sentences:

Yo hablo español todos los días.

I speak Spanish every day.

Ellos hablan muy rápido.

They speak very fast.

Present Tense: -ER Verbs

Example: comer (to eat)

Subject Ending Conjugation Translation
yo -o como I eat
tú -es comes you eat (informal)
él/ella/usted -e come he/she eats, you eat (formal)
nosotros/nosotras -emos comemos we eat
vosotros/vosotras -éis coméis you all eat (informal, Spain)
ellos/ellas/ustedes -en comen they eat, you all eat (formal/Latin America)

Example Sentences:

Yo como frutas y verduras.

I eat fruits and vegetables.

Nosotros comemos juntos los domingos.

We eat together on Sundays.

Present Tense: -IR Verbs

Example: vivir (to live)

Subject Ending Conjugation Translation
yo -o vivo I live
tú -es vives you live (informal)
él/ella/usted -e vive he/she lives, you live (formal)
nosotros/nosotras -imos vivimos we live
vosotros/vosotras -ís vivís you all live (informal, Spain)
ellos/ellas/ustedes -en viven they live, you all live (formal/Latin America)

Example Sentences:

Yo vivo en Madrid.

I live in Madrid.

Ellos viven en un apartamento pequeño.

They live in a small apartment.

Irregular Verbs in the Present Tense

Many common Spanish verbs are irregular, meaning they don't follow the standard conjugation patterns.

Common Irregular Verbs

Infinitive yo tú él/ella/usted nosotros/as vosotros/as ellos/ellas/ustedes
ser (to be) soy eres es somos sois son
estar (to be) estoy estás está estamos estáis están
ir (to go) voy vas va vamos vais van
tener (to have) tengo tienes tiene tenemos tenéis tienen
hacer (to do/make) hago haces hace hacemos hacéis hacen

Example Sentences with Irregular Verbs:

Yo soy estudiante.

I am a student.

Tú estás en casa.

You are at home.

Ellos van al cine.

They go to the movies.

Past Tense Conjugation (Pretérito Perfecto Simple)

The preterite tense is used to talk about completed actions in the past.

-AR Verbs
-ER/-IR Verbs

Preterite Tense: -AR Verbs

Example: hablar (to speak)

Subject Ending Conjugation Translation
yo -é hablé I spoke
tú -aste hablaste you spoke (informal)
él/ella/usted -ó habló he/she spoke, you spoke (formal)
nosotros/nosotras -amos hablamos we spoke
vosotros/vosotras -asteis hablasteis you all spoke (informal, Spain)
ellos/ellas/ustedes -aron hablaron they spoke, you all spoke (formal/Latin America)

Example Sentences:

Yo hablé con mi amigo ayer.

I spoke with my friend yesterday.

Ellos hablaron durante horas.

They spoke for hours.

Preterite Tense: -ER/-IR Verbs

Examples: comer (to eat) and vivir (to live)

Subject Ending comer vivir Translation
yo -í comí viví I ate / I lived
tú -iste comiste viviste you ate / you lived (informal)
él/ella/usted -ió comió vivió he/she ate, you ate (formal) / he/she lived, you lived (formal)
nosotros/nosotras -imos comimos vivimos we ate / we lived
vosotros/vosotras -isteis comisteis vivisteis you all ate / you all lived (informal, Spain)
ellos/ellas/ustedes -ieron comieron vivieron they ate, you all ate (formal/Latin America) / they lived, you all lived (formal/Latin America)

Example Sentences:

Yo comí paella ayer.

I ate paella yesterday.

Ellos vivieron en Barcelona por cinco años.

They lived in Barcelona for five years.

Future Tense Conjugation (Futuro Simple)

The future tense is used to talk about actions that will happen in the future. Unlike the present and preterite tenses, the future tense uses the same endings for all three verb types (-ar, -er, -ir).

Subject Ending hablar comer vivir
yo -é hablaré comeré viviré
tú -ás hablarás comerás vivirás
él/ella/usted -á hablará comerá vivirá
nosotros/nosotras -emos hablaremos comeremos viviremos
vosotros/vosotras -éis hablaréis comeréis viviréis
ellos/ellas/ustedes -án hablarán comerán vivirán

Example Sentences:

Yo hablaré con el profesor mañana.

I will speak with the teacher tomorrow.

Nosotros comeremos en el restaurante.

We will eat at the restaurant.

Ellos vivirán en España el próximo año.

They will live in Spain next year.
