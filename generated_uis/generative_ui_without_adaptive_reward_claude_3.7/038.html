Macroscopic Properties

Hydrogen Bonding
water_drop
Water molecules form hydrogen bonds between the oxygen of one molecule and hydrogen of another. This gives water its unique properties like high boiling point, surface tension, and capillary action.
Polarity and Solubility
bolt
Water's bent molecular shape creates a polar molecule with partial charges. This makes water an excellent solvent for other polar compounds and ionic substances, explaining why it's often called the "universal solvent."
Density Anomaly
ac_unit
Unlike most substances, water's solid form (ice) is less dense than its liquid form. This is due to the hydrogen bonding network that forms in ice, creating a more open crystalline structure.
