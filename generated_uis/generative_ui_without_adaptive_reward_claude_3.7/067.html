Wound Cleaning Procedure

Original (English)

Wound Cleaning Procedure

Proper wound cleaning is essential to prevent infection and promote healing. Follow these steps to clean a wound effectively:

  1. Wash your hands thoroughly with soap and clean water.
  2. Put on clean, disposable gloves if available.
  3. Rinse the wound with clean, room temperature water to remove dirt and debris.
  4. Clean around the wound with mild soap and water, working from the center outward.
  5. Avoid putting soap directly into the wound as it can irritate the tissue.
  6. For deeper wounds, use sterile saline solution to irrigate the wound if available.
  7. Gently pat the area dry with a clean cloth or gauze.
  8. Apply an appropriate topical antiseptic if available.
  9. Cover the wound with a sterile dressing or bandage.

Monitor the wound for signs of infection, including increased redness, swelling, warmth, pain, or discharge. Seek medical attention if these symptoms develop or if the wound is not healing properly.

Cultural Adaptation Notes:

The illustration should be adapted to show indigenous people from Guatemala with appropriate clothing. Medical supplies should reflect what's commonly available in rural clinics (basic gauze, locally available antiseptic plants, etc.).
