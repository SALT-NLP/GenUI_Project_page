Decolonial Ethnographic Research Framework

For research on tourism impacts on indigenous communities in Northern Thailand

Researcher Positionality

Recognizing your position as a Western researcher is an important first step. This section helps you continually reflect on power dynamics, privilege, and how your background shapes your research.

1 Positionality Self-Assessment

    • Reflect on your position: How might your background influence how you interpret what you observe?

    2 Power Dynamics Awareness

    Consider how these aspects of your identity might create power imbalances:

    Economic privilege:
    ? Consider how economic differences between you and community members might influence relationships and information sharing
    Academic status:
    ? Reflect on how your academic position might create perceived expertise or authority
    Language differences:
    ? Consider how language barriers might affect power dynamics and your ability to understand nuance
    Cultural knowledge:
    ? Acknowledge gaps in your cultural understanding that could lead to misinterpretations

    Decolonial Research Resources

    These resources can help deepen your understanding of decolonial methodologies specific to Southeast Asia and indigenous research.
    Decolonizing Methodologies: Research and Indigenous Peoples
    Linda Tuhiwai Smith

    Foundational text on conducting research with indigenous communities that challenges Western research paradigms.

    Research is Ceremony: Indigenous Research Methods
    Shawn Wilson

    Explores indigenous research paradigms and relational accountability in research.

    Indigenous Research Methodologies in Tourism
    Freya Higgins-Desbiolles & Gabrielle Russell-Mundine

    Tourism-specific approaches to indigenous research methods.

    Reflexivity in Tourism Research
    C. Michael Hall

    Frameworks for reflexive practice in tourism ethnography.

    Indigenous Knowledge and Tourism in Southeast Asia
    Huong T. Bui & Alexander Trupp

    Region-specific exploration of indigenous knowledge systems and tourism.

    The Politics of Tourism in Southeast Asia
    Michael Hitchcock, Victor T. King & Michael Parnwell

    Analysis of tourism development and its political implications in Southeast Asia.

    Ethical Research with Indigenous Communities
    AIATSIS

    Guidelines for ethical research practice with indigenous communities.

    Tourism and Indigenous Peoples: Issues and Implications
    Richard Butler & Tom Hinch

    Examines relationships between indigenous peoples and tourism development.
